# Session 2026-03-15 — Paper Update & Consistent Paper-Scope Synthesis

## 1. Paper-Scope Testbench Fix (dwn_top_paper_scope co-simulation)

**Problem**: `test_dwn_paper_scope_fullsim.py` had 1% pass rate (2/200 transactions).

**Root cause**: Driver/monitor throughput mismatch. Driver advanced 1 txn/cycle but monitor waited PIPELINE_DEPTH=4 cycles per txn. After txn#0, every subsequent monitor read was offset by (N × 3) cycles.

**Fix** (`dwn_paper_scope_uvm_tb.py`):
- Driver: `await RisingEdge(dut.clk)` → `await ClockCycles(dut.clk, PIPELINE_DEPTH)` (wait 4 cycles per txn)
- Monitor: `await ClockCycles(dut.clk, PIPELINE_DEPTH)` → `await ClockCycles(dut.clk, 1)` (1 cycle to cross NBA boundary)

**Result**: `test_dwn_paper_scope_fullsim.py` PASSES — 100% RTL==SW for mnist_n2. Architect-verified.

## 4. Key Insights

### WAFR packing is fan-in dependent
- **n≤5**: Neurons map to LUT5, WAFR packs pairs into LUT6_2 → significant area reduction
- **n=6**: Neurons fill complete LUT6, no WAFR pair packing → behavioral ≈ structural
- **MNIST n=6 exception**: 11.4% of neurons have <6 unique inputs (LearnableMapping collisions) → still benefits from WAFR

### Synth-only vs full-impl LUT counts differ
- MNIST n=6 paper-scope: synth-only = 2,436, full-impl = 2,655 (P&R adds ~9%)
- Reason: post-route optimization can both merge AND inflate LUTs depending on congestion

### NID GroupSum overhead
- Pipelined GroupSum adds ~300 fixed LUTs (verified: 501 paper-scope − 201 LUT-stack = 300)
- For tiny NID (508 neurons), this dominates the area — makes DiffLogic (293) more area-efficient
- Non-pipelined GroupSum would be smaller but wasn't synthesized
