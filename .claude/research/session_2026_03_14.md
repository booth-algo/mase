## Part 1: INDEX_BITS Bug Discovery and Fix

### Root Cause
`passes.py` packed input indices into 8-bit fields (`& 0xFF`). MNIST has 2,352 thermometer inputs → indices up to 2,351, which require 12 bits. Indices > 255 were silently truncated to 0, so the majority of LUT wiring disappeared — producing artificially low LUT counts and functionally **wrong** RTL.

All datasets were affected: MNIST (thermo=2352), JSC (thermo=3200), KWS (thermo=4080), CIFAR-10 (thermo=6144+), NID (thermo=366 > 255).

### Fix
**`src/mase_components/dwn_layers/passes.py`**: Added `_index_bits_for(input_size) = max(1, ceil(log2(max(input_size, 2))))`, changed packing to use dynamic `index_bits`, added `INDEX_BITS` to `verilog_param`.

**`src/mase_components/dwn_layers/rtl/fixed_dwn_lut_layer.sv`** and **`fixed_dwn_lut_layer_clocked.sv`**: Added `parameter INDEX_BITS = $clog2(INPUT_SIZE > 1 ? INPUT_SIZE : 2)`, updated `INPUT_INDICES` width and index extraction accordingly.

### Verification
New UVM-style cocotb testbench (`dwn_mnist_uvm_tb.py` + `test_dwn_mnist_fullsim.py`): **500/500 MNIST transactions pass** bit-exact between RTL and Python `sw_forward` golden reference.

### Re-synthesis (xcvu9p, beholder0) — fixed RTL
15 configs re-emitted and re-synthesised. Key comparison vs paper (MNIST n=6 "lg"):
- **Before fix**: 1,285 LUTs (3.18× fewer than paper — **wrong**)
- **After fix**: 4,769 LUTs, 5,832 FFs, ~301 MHz (1.17× more than paper — explainable by synthesis strategy)

## Part 2: Fmax Gap — Paper-Scope Synthesis

### Paper Scope Confirmed
From arXiv:2410.11112 Appendix D: Table 2 OOC synthesis **excludes the thermometer encoder**. Only LUT layers + GroupSum are synthesised. Strategy: `Flow_PerfOptimized_high` at highest achievable frequency.

### Root Cause of 301 MHz Fmax
`full_pipeline_top_clocked` used **combinational GroupSum** (`$countones` on 1000 bits → 7-level adder tree). Vivado absorbed the inter-layer FFs (neurons are trivial 1-LUT, WAFR-packed), making GroupSum the single critical path.

### Fix: `dwn_top_paper_scope.sv`
New top-level (no thermometer) wrapping:
1. `dwn_top_clocked` — LUT stack with inter-layer pipeline FFs
2. `fixed_dwn_groupsum_pipelined` — **2-stage pipelined GroupSum** (sub-group popcounts Stage 1 → sum Stage 2, each registered)

New synthesis script `scripts/synth_paper_match.tcl` uses `Flow_PerfOptimized_high` directives:
`PerformanceOptimized` synth → `ExploreWithRemap` opt → `ExtraPostPlacementOpt` place → `AggressiveExplore` phys_opt → `AggressiveExplore` route.

### Synthesis Sweep (xcvu9p, beholder0)

| Clock target | LUTs | FFs | Fmax |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.21 ns (827 MHz) | 2,594 | 1,742 | 765 MHz |
| **1.15 ns (869 MHz)** | **2,655** | **1,752** | **791 MHz ← best** |

| **Paper (Table 2)** | **4,082** | **3,385** | **827 MHz** |

### Result vs Paper

| Metric | Ours | Paper | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|-------|
| LUTs | **2,655** | 4,082 | 35% fewer — WAFR packing of behavioral RTL |
| FFs | **1,752** | 3,385 | 48% fewer — WAFR + pruned dead bits |
| Fmax | **791 MHz** | 827 MHz | **95.6% of paper — 4.4% gap** |

**Fmax improvement: 301 MHz → 791 MHz (2.63×).**

### Remaining Gap Explanation
- **LUT gap**: Paper uses FloPoCo structural LUT6 instantiations → no WAFR packing → 4,082 physical LUTs. MASE behavioral RTL → WAFR → 2,655 LUTs. MASE is more area-efficient.
- **Fmax gap**: Architect confirmed critical path is GroupSum Stage 2 (5 logic levels, 1.245 ns data path). FloPoCo experiment (in synthesis_tracker.md) showed 3-stage pipeline achieves 837.5 MHz. Our 2-stage → 791 MHz.
